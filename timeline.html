Major Events of the Beatles' ten-year run

When he was just a teenager, John Lennon formed a group called the Quarrymen with several friends, including Stuart Sutcliffe. The group was later joined by Paul McCartney and his friend George Harrison. They first called themselves the Silver Beetles after the group, Buddy Holly and the Crickets, then shortened it to the Beetles and eventually the Beatles.

The group hired Pete Best as their full-time drummer. Former bassist Stuart Sutcliff left the group in pursuit of an art scholarship and Paul took over on bass. They established a two-year residency in Hamburg, Germany, playing different clubs throughout the city.

They began playing on home turf again in Liverpool's renowned Cavern Club. Brian Epstein first met the group at the club and soon became their manager. The drummer, Pete Best left the group and was replaced by Ringo Starr.

Their first studio album Please Please Me was released. "Beatlemania" begins around this time.

The group took the U.S.A. by storm (as part of the British invasion) and made broadcast TV history by appearing on the Ed Sullivan Show, a popular variety show, in early February. They starred in their first film, "A Hard Day's Night", based off their album of the same name.

The Beatles starred in the second popular film, "Help!" also based off the same album. It was filmed in color this time. The concert at New York's Shea Stadium, now torn down and rebuilt as Citi Field, marked the beginning of their North American tour. The roar of the crowd was so deafening, the members could hardly hear their own music!

The Beatles retire from touring after their Candlestick Park concert in San Francisco. They create the rest of their music in the studio. The group comes under fire when John Lennon claims they're "...more popular than Jesus."

The music beging to take on a psychedelic feel with the release of the Sgt. Pepper album. Paul once explained that the concept of Sgt. Pepper's band is like the Beatles' alter ego. Using that persona, they experimented with different sounds and created music that was groundbreaking at the time. Their manager, Brian Epstein, passes away from a drug overdose.

Due to Harrison being influneced by Hinduism, the group traveled to India to study meditation under a guru named Maharishi Mahesh Yogi. An animated movie, "Yellow Submarine" is released. Although the Beatles characters were not actually voiced by their real-life counterparts, the real Beatles made a cameo appearance at the end scene.

Tension within the group becomes evident and continues to rise during the production of their Abbey Road album.

On January 30th, The group performs one last concert on the rooftop of Apple Headquarters in London. Eventually, the police order them to stop playing and come down.The Beatles split up once Paul departs from the group in April and John leaves in September. Each of the members pursue solo work.
